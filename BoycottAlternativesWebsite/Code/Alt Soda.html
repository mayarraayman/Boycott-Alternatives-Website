Pepsi

Pepsi is a carbonated soft drink manufactured by PepsiCo. Originally created and developed in 1893 by Caleb Bradham and introduced as Brad's Drink, it was renamed as Pepsi-Cola in 1898, and then shortened to Pepsi in 1961. In 2022, it was the second most valuable soft drink brand worldwide, behind Coca-Cola.

Coca-Cola

On May 8, 1886, Dr. John Pemberton brought his perfected syrup to Jacobs' Pharmacy in downtown Atlanta where the first glass of Coca‑Cola was poured.

Spirou Spats

"Spirou Spats" company was established in the 1920s, specifically in 1920, when it started producing the first carbonated water bottle in Egypt under the name "Spats Lemonade." Over time, Spats products were able to conquer the markets by offering high-quality production that gained acceptance in all social circles. Over the years, Spats has evolved to offer a variety of soft drinks, mineral water, and juices in different flavors and sizes. The company has been able to meet the needs and preferences of consumers, establishing its position as a leading beverage company in Egypt.
